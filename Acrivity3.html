Basic Structure of a Computer

A typical computer consists of hardware and software. The hardware includes the physical components such as the Central Processing Unit (CPU), memory (RAM), storage (hard drives or SSDs), and peripherals like keyboards and monitors. The software consists of the operating system (like Windows, macOS, or Linux) and various applications that allow users to interact with the hardware and perform specific tasks.

Functions of Computers: Data Processing: Computers can process vast amounts of data quickly, whether it's performing calculations, running simulations, or analyzing information. Storage: Computers store enormous amounts of data, from documents and images to databases and entire programs, enabling quick retrieval and manipulation. Communication: They connect people across the world via the internet, enabling email, video calls, and social media interactions. Automation: In industries, computers are used to automate tasks, reducing human error and improving efficiency.
